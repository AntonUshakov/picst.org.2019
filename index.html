October 8 - 11, 2019 Ukraine, Kharkiv

International Scientific and Practical Conference

«Problems of Infocommunications.Science and Technology»

8 - 11 October, 2019 on the basis of Kharkiv National University of Radio Electronics will be held the International Scientific and Practical Conference «Problems of Infocommunications. Science and Technology» (PICS&T-2018).

The purpose of the conference - the exchange of information on theoretical, scientific and practical achievements, as well as generalization and systematization of the results and experience of implementation of scientific research in the development of new information and communication technologies and planning of modern infocommunication systems.

    • Requirements for the paper

    All papers submitted to the conference must not have been published, submitted or accepted for publication elsewhere. IEEE policy regarding plagiarism and duplicate submission/publication will be strictly enforced. All papers will be judged on the basis of their clarity, relevance, originality, and contribution. Manuscripts will be limited to 6 (IEEE style) pages.

    Size of the conference paper should be 4 to 6 full A4 pages, prepared according IEEE Proceedings format.

    Figures should be black and white. When making figures in the document it is not allowed to use the Microsoft Word Drawing toolbar. It is recommended to insert a figure from the clipboard or a file without being tied to a photo editor.

    An Word template of paper can be downloaded here

    Papers that are not formatted properly won’t be accepted for review process.

    Papers should be submitted electronically via the on-line submission system . Paper submission should follow the IEEE doublecolumn format for conferences (templates will be available via the conference web site). Submitted papers will undergo a Double Blind peer review process coordinated by the Program Committee and send to two independent anonymous referee review. Authors of papers will be notified by e-mail about reviewing results. All submissions must be English and meet Guidelines for Double Blind Review.

    For participation in the conference submit the following:

    filled application online;

    a Conference Paper Draft Manuscript in PDF format designed in accordance with the attached requirements you can upload online during submission (If you have any troubles with paper submission, please inform Organizing Committee: conf@picst.org);

    Copyright Form (filled online after acceptance);

    Print Ready Paper in MS Word and PDF format (submitted after acceptance);

    expert conclusion (for staff of NURE) filled in Information Security Department. Copy of signed expert conclusion must be send to conf@picst.org

    All submitted papers are subjects to two independent anonymous referee review. Authors of papers will be notified by e-mail about reviewing results.

    Working languages: English. It will be used for all printed materials, presentations and discussions.

    Organizing Committee warns that the conference papers will not be subject to editing literary, so responsibility for spelling, punctuation and stylistic errors relies on authors.

    Authors of papers, caused the greatest interest will be invited to publish articles in scientific journal, registered in Higher Attestation Commission of Ukraine "Problems of telecommunications" (http://pt.journal.kh.ua).

    Workflow:

    • create an account/ log in on EasyChair website (EasyChair instructions for Authors);
    • submit application and Conference Paper Draft Manuscript(EasyChair instructions for Authors);
    • Double Blind peer review process (Guidelines for Double Blind Review);
    • Notification of autors about reviewing results;
    • review response (rebuttal) phase;
    • accepted papers list publication;
    • Submit print ready paper;
    • Open conference registration;
    • Create conference program;
    • Visiting of PIC S&T`2019 Conference.

    Guidelines

    EasyChair instructions for Authors

    Guidelines for Double Blind Review

    Pay attention on filling fields of Submission Form. Information from this form will used for Contents and Authors Index then submission to IEEE Xplore.

    Author name

    “First Name” must be full, not initials. Both “First Name” and “Last Name” must be same as Author’s Scopus and ORCID profile.

    Organization

    “Organization” must be full, not abbreviation, correct official name in English (look Organization official website for correct information), without Department and location (city, country, postal address).

    Email

    You must use the same email address that you signed up with when creating the EasyChair account.

    Title capitalization

    You must use correct title capitalization (use Title Capitalization tool (APA style): https://capitalizemytitle.com/).

    Capitalize the first and the last word. All words four letters or more get capitalized, as do any nouns, pronouns, verbs, or adjectives. Any hyphenated word will require a capital letter after the hyphen in titles (for instance, Pre-University). Lowercase articles (a, an, the), coordinating conjunctions, and prepositions. Lowercase the ‘to’ in an infinitive.

    Abstract

    Abstracts should be a single paragraph. By nature, Abstracts shall not contain numbered mathematical equations nor numbered references. Numbered reference citations are not allowed. If a citation is made, reword the sentence to exclude citation numbers. In order for an abstract to be effective when displayed in IEEE Xplore® as well as through indexing services such as Compendex, INSPEC, Medline, ProQuest, and Web of Science, it must be an accurate, stand-alone reflection of the contents of the article. The abstract must be a concise yet comprehensive reflection of what is in your article. In particular:

    • The abstract must be self-contained, without abbreviations, footnotes, or references. It should be a microcosm of the full article.
    • The abstract must be between 150-250 words. Be sure that you adhere to these limits; otherwise, you will need to edit your abstract accordingly.
    • The abstract must be written as one paragraph, and should not contain displayed mathematical equations or tabular material.
    • The abstract should include three or four different keywords or phrases, as this will help readers to find it. It is important to avoid over-repetition of such phrases as this can result in a page being rejected by search engines.
    • Ensure that your abstract reads well and is grammatically correct.
